The Failing Village

Being the story of Wren Tobin, who came home to a village the river had forgotten,
and found what her family had hidden in the woods

An Illustrated Tale in Four Acts · With Four Endings

Three years after she left, Wren Tobin walks the east road home to a village the river has forgotten. Her father is dead. His mill is still. What he left her — wrapped in oilcloth, tied in a hesitating knot — is a journal of names: Field Cap, Goldfoot, Brightspore, Wendlight. In Hollowfen, knowing the true name of a thing can feed a family, save a life, and unbury what powerful men worked hard to make a village forget.

“Never eat what you cannot name twice.”

— Tobin's journal, underlined until the pencil tore the page
